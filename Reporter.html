Reporter System

Once we have the results from either the transcriptomics or the phage display, we’ll have to be able to see what we’ve done and give a readout of how many worms are present! We used fluorescent chromoproteins to be able to detect our worms in both grass and in feces. This potentially allows a relatively simple and quantitative way for ranchers to detect our worms of interest, using a cheap UV lamp, a dark room, and a camera!

In Poop!


Below you can see colonies expressing GFP in horse poop, showing that our reporter system is potentially viable and that GFP can be indeed seen in horse feces.

In Grass!


To detect largestrongyles in grass, we expressed GFP producing cells on grass to prove their visibility.
